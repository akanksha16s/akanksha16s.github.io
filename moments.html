Something About Those Moments Stayed

Reflections on Udaipur and the Palani Hills

There are some moments you don’t realise are important when they are happening. They just sit quietly somewhere in your memory, and later you notice—they changed something.

One such moment happened in Udaipur. We were staying inside the Sajjangarh Wildlife Sanctuary for a conference. It was late at night, and I couldn’t sleep. That phase after COVID… I used to have a lot of anxiety. So I stepped out, just to walk, thinking it might help.

The place was quiet in a very different way. Not empty—just still. After walking for a while, I saw a few guards standing on a slightly raised structure. It wasn’t exactly a terrace, but high enough to look over the area. It felt odd that they were just standing there, so I went closer and climbed up.

And then I saw it. A tiger.

It was just there. Standing. Not doing anything. I don’t remember feeling scared. That’s what I find strange even now. It was such a powerful animal, right there, and yet there was no panic in me. I was just… still.

The way it stood—calm, almost effortless. Like everything around it already belonged to it, and it didn’t need to prove it. It felt like the knight of the jungle had come to visit his own territory."

It looked around slowly, and then at one point, it looked up—towards us. I don’t know how long that moment lasted. Probably a few seconds. But I remember my eyes filling up. Not because I was overwhelmed in a dramatic way, but because something about it felt too real. Too complete.

***

A few months later, something else happened. I was in the forests of the Palani Hills, near Madurai. We were just passing through, and what stood out immediately was that the space still belonged to the animals.

Then I saw a baby monkey. It was sitting in its mother’s lap. Very small. Just watching. I don’t know why, but I stopped. It kept looking at me, and then it tapped its mother and pointed towards us. It kept looking back and forth, like it was trying to understand what we were—two-legged creatures with different, almost strange skin.

And in that moment, something felt very clear. The jungle had expressed itself to me. I was no longer the one observing—it was me who was being seen, understood, maybe even questioned. I felt… honoured. Like I had been allowed into something that wasn’t mine.

I also realised something else that day—maybe the people who live closer to nature, the ones we often call “less sophisticated,” understand these relationships far better than we ever will. No matter how much we try, there are some connections we have already lost.

Being someone who doesn’t really believe in religion or conventional ideas of God, these moments made me question the kind of world we are living in. No one really knows what spirituality is. No one really knows what religion is. And yet, we fight over it.

I think if there is a god, it is probably like that. Not loud. Not demanding. Not dividing. Just there. Existing.And maybe, that is where the real power lies. In simple existance...

Just like this place of mine on the internet… quietly existing.
