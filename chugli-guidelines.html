Chugli: Official Guidelines

Chugli counts a person's weaknesses or constraints behind his/her back[cite: 2]. It enables the chugalkhor to release negative energy, reducing mental agony and fostering emotional stability[cite: 3].

The All-India Chugli Association (AICA) promotes chugli as an emotional connection, distinct from "back bitching," which is considered rude and derogatory[cite: 4, 7, 8, 10].

  • Never do chugli about serious issues like diseases or family setbacks[cite: 12].
  • Do not interrupt the flow of a chugalkhor[cite: 13, 14].
  • Never offer logical solutions to the problems discussed[cite: 15].
  • Chugli is private; it must stay within the group[cite: 16].
  • Never reveal the chugli to the subject involved; it hampers their self-confidence[cite: 17, 18, 19].
