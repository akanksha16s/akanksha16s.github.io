Chugli: Official Guidelines

Chugli counts a person's weaknesses or constraints behind his/her back. It enables the chugalkhor to release all its negative energy towards the person in front of a third person, reducing mental agony and fostering emotional stability.

The All-India Chugli Association (AICA) has decided to define guidelines for the act of chugli. These rules aim to create awareness amongst the people involved in the act of chugli so that, together, we can create an atmosphere of healthy chugli.

Furthermore, there is the English term ‘back bitching’, which is believed to be a synonym for chugli. However, the AICA wants to clarify a very basic difference between the two. The latter is more of an emotion; it enables a strong emotional connection between two chugalkhors. In chugli, we do not hate the person whose chugli is being done. However, bitching sounds rude and derogatory because the root word is ‘bitch’.

Therefore, the AICA promotes chugli, but in no sort of way do we promote bitching.

Rules

  • Never do chugli about serious issues like diseases or family setbacks.
  • Do not interrupt the flow of a chugalkhor.
  • Never offer logical solutions to the problems discussed.
  • Chugli is a private and intimate affair; it should not be leaked outside the group.
  • Never reveal the chugli to the subject involved; it hampers their self-confidence.
